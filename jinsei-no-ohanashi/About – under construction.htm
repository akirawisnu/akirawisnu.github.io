About

I am a PhD Researcher in Development Policy at Global Development Institute, The University of Manchester.

I am currently doing research on “Economic and Political Inclusion and the Role of Anti-Poverty Programmes in Indonesia”. The project aims to critically evaluate the conceptual basis of inclusion and examine the inclusiveness of anti-poverty programmes. The research is fully funded by Indonesia Endowment Fund for Education. Other than research, I also work as Graduate Teaching Assistant in Economic Development courses.

My specializations are  in the issue of inclusion, labor market discrimination, and social protection. My other research interests include anti-poverty transfers, microfinance, and financial inclusion for development.

Prior to embarking PhD, I had worked in various research projects related to poverty and impact assessment of social protection programmes in Indonesia. I worked closely with both government and non-government agencies. I have experiences in both quantitative (e.g. econometric analysis of household dataset, survey) and qualitative research projects (e.g. designing data collection tools, conducting interviews, and field work).

I hold degrees in Accounting and Economics from Ritsumeikan Asia Pacific University and Development Economics and Policy from The University of Manchester. Throughout the studies, I have been awarded various awards and scholarships including Japan’s Monbukagakusho Scholarship, ITOCHU scholarship, Ando Momofuku scholarship, Best graduate thesis, and so forth.

Besides work and research, I invest a lot of time in doing zumba and enjoy spending time with friends, cooking, learning piano, singing, rambling around the countryside, and capturing moments through photography. Some of my latest photos are here.
