About Me

Family

I was born in Jakarta, August 25, 1989, and happened to be the only daughter of my parents, well, I still have two brothers actually. My dad is a public servant, working in the Ministry of Home Affairs, on the one side, my mom works in the Indonesian air force. Talking about my older brother, he’s currently studying in the air force academy, following the path of my mom and hope to graduate very soon in the mid of December this year. And, the last but not least, my little brother is now currently enrolled in a public high school as a freshmen this year. Upps, and me myself, Im currently studying abroad in an international university in Japan since 2007.

Childhood

I guess, I might have a pretty similar childhood with all of you in which I love watching cartoons, love to sing, playing with friends, and other stuff. But let me share you my best memories of my childhood started from Kindergarten. Since I was really young, I don’t know, but I just love competition. It’s just so funny if I remember the first time I learned how to read and write. By that time, I was only three, and I hardly insisted my parents to teach me how to read simply because my brother just newly enrolled in the kindergarten and knew how to read. After finished the kindergarten, then I continued to enroll for elementary level of education. For the first three years, I enrolled in a public school where a table was set for three students, so there were approximately 70 students (normally 30-40) in the class. Till now, I couldn’t even imagine how I could survive. However, I found out myself to do something great at that time. A condition in which almost of my friends had a lot of money in their pocket to buy foods in school while I didn’t, encouraged me to search additional pocket money by selling candies in the class. I still remember also that I could even hire a two or three of my friends and waged them; an extraordinary thing that I could recognized from my childhood. Unfortunately, my small business stopped after we moved out to live in halim and I had to spend another three years to complete my study. My enrollment in a private school here taught me a lot of things and gave me such wonderful experiences. More facilities helped me to stimulate my interests in dancing, cheer-leading, and being a doctor. However, still my sense of competitions had colored my life at that time. I was one of top students with extra ordinary achievements in extracurricular, being a leader for project assignments, elected to be dokter kecil (kind of medical staff), and assigned to be a captain for cheerleader team. Graduated from school, I dreamed to go for one of the best JHS in the area but I couldn’t make it as my final year examination wasn’t enough. So that I revenged and finally could make it for HS, I enrolled in the best public school to be named SMAN 8 in the capital city of Indonesia, Jakarta. As smart people were gathered here, competitions were tough, but still I could present achievements to my HS in the field of social research and debate competitions.

Dreams and Interest

As I mentioned before, I love singing, dancing, leadership, social research, entrepreneurship, so forth. But none of them attracted me a lot to be a professional in one of those fields. Rather, I was more excited to be a doctor. Yea a doctor which was my childhood dream for about 10 years ago. However, it was in catastrophe 2 years ago. It wasn’t because I couldn’t make it to enter the medical college, I did, I was accepted in a university to major in medical studies without any entrance examination because of my extraordinary achievements and excellent academic record. But at the same time, unexpected encouragement to go for study abroad was uncontrolled after I got a scholarship in the university that I am currently enrolled now. Yes, that’s the turnover point of my life, I believe Allah has shown the path for the best of my life. Now, I am no longer pursuing my childhood dream, rather, I am pursuing my brand new dreams. Someday in the future, I truly want to make my parents and my country, Indonesia, proud of having me. I have passion in economics, educations, and Islam and struggling to be a professional in the future. For details on my current activities, please refer to my profile.

From a friend of yours,

Virgi Agita Sari
